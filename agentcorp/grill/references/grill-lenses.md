# Grill Lenses

Aim each question with one lens; use only the lenses that fit this plan. Never dump the list on the owner — the iron law holds: one question at a time, at the current weakest point.

- **Goal reality** — What user, operational, or business pain is this solving? Who feels it today?
- **Success signal** — What observable result proves it worked? What would you measure a month in?
- **Non-goal boundary** — What tempting adjacent problem is intentionally out of scope, and can you say why?
- **Evidence** — What fact, if false, would change the decision? Has anyone checked it?
- **Knowledge matrix** — Which load-bearing items are known knowns, known unknowns, unknown knowns (recorded somewhere but not in this frame), or still unnamed?
- **Counterexample** — What input, user, environment, or timing case breaks the plan?
- **Simpler alternative** — What smaller move would get most of the value? Why isn't it enough?
- **Cost of wrongness** — If this bet is wrong, what becomes expensive to undo?
- **Dependency** — What team, service, permission, data, or tool must be true for this to work?
- **User behavior** — What must people do differently, and why would they actually do it?
- **Verification** — What test, check, or observation would catch the failure early?

When an answer converts an unknown, name its resolution path on the spot: ask the sponsor, inspect the repo, search prior artifacts, research externally (`parallel-researcher`), run a cheap experiment, or accept an explicit assumption with a risk owner and revisit point.
